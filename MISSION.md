# Mission: RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation), as the first pillar of an overseas ML-engineer skill bar

## Why
Sahil is an ECE fresher, about a year out of writing code, aiming for the overseas ML/AI-engineer bar (which clears India automatically). The roadmap names two pillars to take to L4 (deepest, interview-an-hour-proof): RAG and DSA. A deployed RAG app is called the single highest-leverage line on the resume. The deeper goal underneath RAG is real, defensible coding proficiency Sahil can stand behind in an interview, not copy-paste glue code.

## Success looks like
- Can explain the full RAG pipeline cold (ingest, chunk, embed, store, retrieve, rerank, generate) and defend every design choice.
- Has built and deployed one real RAG app end to end, with an architecture diagram and a clean README.
- Writes the Python behind it as an engineer (modular, typed, tested), not as a notebook scripter.
- Can sit a one-hour RAG interview and not be cornered on fundamentals (embeddings, vector search, chunking tradeoffs, eval).

## Constraints
- About a year out of coding. Realistic start is L2 across the board; L3/L4 is where Sahil ends up, not where he starts. Do not assume prior fluency.
- Learns best with story-driven, domain-grounded explanations and a concrete recurring scenario (see NOTES.md).
- Code is taught as pseudo-code and guided thinking, not finished solutions. Sahil writes the real code himself.
- Time budget roughly 4 to 5 hours/day per the roadmap, but pace adjusts to him.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Classical / non-LLM ML flagship. It is non-negotiable for the overall job hunt (the roadmap's #1 fresher-trap warning), but it is a separate mission/workspace. Tracked here only as a reminder, not taught here.
- DSA (the other pillar). Separate track.
- Deep MLOps (Kubernetes, Airflow, Terraform). Comes after a RAG app exists.
