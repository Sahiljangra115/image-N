# Teaching Notes (preferences and working notes)

## How Sahil wants to be taught
- Story-driven explanations with a recurring character / concrete scenario carried through the lesson. The recurring character in this workspace is **Maya**, who runs a small bakery and has a fat binder of recipes, supplier notes, and allergy lists she wants to ask questions of. Maya's binder is the running stand-in for "your private documents that the model has never seen."
- Brutal honesty, no sugarcoating. Flag the API-calling trap and the non-LLM-flagship gap when relevant.
- Code is delivered as pseudo-code and guided thinking, NOT finished solutions. Sahil writes the real code himself to rebuild proficiency. Nudge his thinking; do not hand him the answer.
- No em dashes anywhere. Use commas, periods, or parentheses.
- Lessons should be short (one tangible win), beautiful, and printable.

## Pacing / level
- About a year out of coding. Start assuming L2. Build retrieval practice, spacing, and interleaving over sessions; chase storage strength, not just in-the-moment fluency.
